Course Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

  • Identify the key terms, concepts, and theories of consumer behaviour
  • Evaluate the principal theories of consumer behaviour; critically assess strengths, limitations, and applications
  • Apply consumer behaviour concepts to real world marketing problems and develop better marketing programs and strategies to influence those behaviours
  • Analyze the current trends in consumer behaviour; and apply them to the marketing of an actual product or service
  • Use consumer behaviour methods and basic data-analytic tools to inform decision-making within the context of real-world marketing problems

Course Policies

Course Expectations

  • Learning takes effort. Completing the reading, coming to class, participating, and studying all play an important role in your success.
  • I'm on your team. My goal is to help you learn, not to catch you making mistakes, so please ask questions and reach out if you need support.
  • I know life happens, so I've built flexibility into many parts of this course. That flexibility is built in for everyone, and additional accommodations are generally reserved for exceptional circumstances.

Participation

  • You'll get the most out of this course by coming to class. The readings and slides are important, but they aren't a substitute for the discussions, activities, examples, and connections we make together.
  • If your course includes team activities or projects, your teammates are counting on you to contribute.
  • Participation includes flexibility for occasional absences. Unless you're going to miss a significant portion of the course, you generally don't need to email me if you're absent.

Late Work

  • Deadlines matter and are intended to help you stay on track throughout the semester.
  • Every assignment includes a 48-hour flexibility window. During this time, you may submit your work without penalty and without contacting me.
  • This flexibility is intended to cover the ordinary challenges of student life, so there's generally no need to request an extension during this period.
  • After the flexibility window, late submissions are accepted but will receive a late penalty. Additional extensions are generally reserved for exceptional circumstances or approved Academic Consideration.

Missed Midterms and Exams

  • Midterms and final exams are expected to be written at the scheduled time.
  • The midterm is a designated assessment and requires supporting documentation for Academic Consideration.
  • If illness or another extenuating circumstance prevents you from writing a midterm or final exam, please submit an Academic Consideration request as soon as possible. Approved requests will be accommodated according to university policy. If you're unsure what to do, please reach out.

Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI)

  • AI is becoming an important part of many workplaces, and learning how to use it thoughtfully is a valuable skill.
  • You're welcome to use AI to support your learning by brainstorming ideas, explaining concepts, creating practice questions, or improving your writing.
  • Use AI to help you learn, not to do your learning for you. Unless otherwise specified, submitted work should reflect your own understanding, analysis, and judgment.
  • If you're ever unsure whether a particular use of AI is appropriate, just ask.

Grades

  • Grades are intended to reflect your demonstrated understanding of the course material and achievement of the learning outcomes.
  • If you believe I have made an error in grading, please let me know. I'm always happy to review my work and correct any mistakes.
  • Requests for extra credit, grade increases, or grade rounding that are not based on a grading error will generally not be granted.
